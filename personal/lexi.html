Lexi K. Cline

Lexi K. Cline was born in Grand Junction, Colorado and grew up in various places in Colorado and Utah. This garnered a love for the mountains, forests, deserts, and she never lacked a place to explore. At a young age, she would doodle and enjoy making up her own characters. As time went on, she developed a talent for art and art history, although she was her own worst critic. An art teacher she once had, liked to teach a lesson of detachment by going to students' works in progress and painting a big black streak across the canvas. She recalls that when he came to her, he told her she ruins her own artwork. Nearing her graduation from high school, she had a choice of paths, and instead of going off to the Navy, she decided to go to college to pursue her art after being convinced by her father, her self-proclaimed biggest fan. After spending a short time at Colorado Mesa University, she transferred to Wright State University in Dayton, Ohio where she majored in Art and Sculpture, with a minor in art history.
